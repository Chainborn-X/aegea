# Complete Main Story

## Full Synopsis

The player is a young wanderer from the coastal village of **Elia**, born with recurring dreams of a mountain crowned in stormlight. After a night of falling stars, ancient ruins awaken across the world. Monsters appear where old temples sleep. The player discovers they are the reincarnation of the final **Pactbearer**, a mortal soul once entrusted with balancing gods, humans, and Primordials.

At first, the journey seems simple: recover divine relics, restore the gods, and prevent the world from falling into chaos.

Over time, the player learns that the gods did not merely protect humanity. They also constrained it. The Sundering was not an accident of evil, but the result of divine fear. The Primordials are not simply destroyers, but suppressed foundations of existence.

The player must gather the **Seven Echoes**, enter the broken axis of Olympus, and decide the shape of the next age.

## Act I - The Sleeping World

### Beginning

The game opens in **Elia**, a fishing village beneath old cliffside ruins.

Opening sequence:

- The player dreams of a child standing before a sealed golden gate.
- A star falls into the nearby **Hearthwood Shrine**.
- Monsters emerge from forgotten catacombs.
- The village elder reveals the player bears an old soul-mark.
- The player finds the first relic: **Ember of Hestia**, which lights sacred braziers and awakens dormant memories.

The player travels across the first region and learns that old myths are physically embedded in the land.

### Act I Goals

- Discover the world has begun remembering itself.
- Awaken the first three divine Echoes.
- Learn that Olympus is sealed.
- Meet **Lysandra**, a temple knight who believes restoring the gods is the only way to save humanity.
- Encounter the first Primordial influence in the ruined city of **Kartha Nemea**.

### Act I Major Reveal

The player is not chosen by the gods.

The player is remembered by the world.

## Act II - The Broken History

The game opens into broader exploration by sea, mountain pass, and ancient underworld routes.

The player visits drowned kingdoms, sky observatories, haunted battlefields, desert libraries, and forgotten workshops. Each region complicates the official myths.

They recover relics tied to faded gods, but the gods themselves appear damaged, partial, or contradictory. Athena remembers guilt. Poseidon remembers rage. Apollo cannot distinguish prophecy from regret. Hades remembers the truth but cannot speak it plainly until the Underworld rivers are restored.

### Act II Goals

- Recover the remaining divine Echoes.
- Decode the language of the First Naming.
- Learn the Sundering was caused by the Aegis Meridian.
- Discover the player's past life helped stop Zeus but failed to save the world.
- Watch Lysandra become increasingly desperate as her faith fractures.
- Meet **Ione**, a scholar-companion who believes history must be faced, not repaired.
- Face **King Melanthos**, a mortal ruler trying to seize divine power to establish a human empire.

### Act II Major Twist

The Primordials are awakening because the old divine bindings are decaying naturally. Restoring Olympus exactly as it was will also restore the bindings, but at the cost of freezing human spiritual evolution.

Creating a new order may free humanity, but risks unleashing forces no one fully understands.

Ending the gods may grant freedom, but it may also end the protection that made civilization possible.

## Act III - The Axis of Memory

The final act begins when the player opens the **Gate of Echoes** beneath Mount Olympus.

World changes:

- ancient roads rise
- hidden islands surface
- the sky reveals fragments of Olympus
- Underworld rivers become traversable
- optional final truths can be uncovered

The player ascends through the broken remains of Olympus, each divine palace now a memory-space reflecting the god's failure, love, and fear.

At the summit, the player confronts:

- **Zeus the Unremembered**, a collapsing god terrified of oblivion.
- **Aion**, Primordial Time, who offers release through recurrence.
- **The player's first incarnation**, an Echo who asks whether mortals are strong enough to live without divine certainty.

## Major Reveals

- The Olympians were born from mortal memory, not separate from it.
- Prometheus's fire came from a Primordial source, which means human freedom predates Olympian rule.
- Many heroic legends are altered histories of suppression, betrayal, and political mythmaking.
- The Sundering was caused by a failed attempt to freeze divine order forever.
- The player once helped oppose the Aegis Meridian, but their failure fractured the world.
- The Primordials are not returning to punish humanity. They are returning because the old bindings are decaying.

## Core Character Arcs

### Player

From local survivor to world witness to maker of an age. The player inherits guilt but is not bound to repeat the first Pactbearer's choices.

### Ione

From detached historian to active guardian of painful truth. She learns that history is not just correction, but care for the living.

### Lysandra

From devout restorer of the old gods to either ally, fanatic, or tragic sacrifice. Her arc depends on whether she can separate faith from control.

### Phaon

From cheerful escape-seeker to someone willing to face grief. His sister's lost voice becomes a symbol of the sea's memory.

### Demeas

From protective elder hiding the past to a mentor who admits that concealment is also a form of harm.

### Melanthos

From compelling human liberator to tyrant-in-the-making. He proves that rejecting gods does not automatically create freedom.

## Ending Paths

### Ending 1 - Restore the Old Order

The player repairs the Aegis Meridian.

Outcome:

- The gods return.
- Monsters recede.
- Temples bloom with power.
- Humanity is protected but guided back into old patterns.
- Lysandra survives and becomes the first new oracle.
- The final image shows children reciting myths exactly as they were written.

Theme: safety at the cost of change.

### Ending 2 - Create a New Order

The player reforges the Pact with gods, mortals, and Primordials all acknowledged.

Outcome:

- The gods return diminished but honest.
- Primordials become part of natural cycles rather than prisoners.
- Magic changes form.
- Humanity must take responsibility for memory, death, nature, and freedom.
- Ione founds the first open archive of true history.
- The final image shows a new constellation forming over Olympus.

Theme: difficult hope.

### Ending 3 - End the Age of Gods

The player dissolves the divine names and releases their Echoes into humanity.

Outcome:

- The gods die as beings but survive as human virtues, arts, songs, and choices.
- Divine magic ends.
- Monsters born of divine wounds vanish.
- Primordial forces become raw nature again.
- The player loses the soul-mark.
- The final image shows mortals climbing Olympus without prayer.

Theme: freedom with grief.

### Secret Ending - The Eighth Echo

If the player uncovers all Primordial sanctums, restores all Underworld names, completes major myth sets, and refuses every absolute answer, they unlock a fourth path.

The player becomes the **First Witness** of a world where gods, mortals, dead, and Primordials can change without ownership.

Outcome:

- No order is final.
- The world remains mysterious.
- The player sails beyond the known map toward lands untouched by Olympus.

Theme: wisdom is not control.

# World Map Design

## Macro Structure

Aegea is built for gradual unlocking. Early travel is local and grounded. Later progression opens sea routes, mountain winds, underworld passages, ancient lifts, and sky fragments.

The world should feel like an archipelago of mysteries connected by memory, myth, and travel mastery.

## Traversal Unlock Philosophy

Progression is Metroidvania-style, but it should feel diegetic:

- An old flame reveals architecture rather than "unlocking red doors."
- A tide conch exposes sea caves and hidden islands.
- A moon charm reveals forest paths that were always present.
- A forge gauntlet lets the player move ancient machinery.
- A lyre restores songs that change time, light, or memory states.
- An underworld obol lets the player cross spirit roads.
- A thunder relic powers ancient pylons and sky bridges.

## Major Regions

### Helikon Vale

Description: Green hills, olive groves, ruined shrines, river caves, and the starting village of Elia.

Mood: Warm, nostalgic, slightly haunted.

Story purpose: Introduces memory, ruins, home, and the first Echo.

Unique mechanics:

- Light sacred braziers.
- Reveal ghostly images of the past.
- Burn thorn seals.
- Open root doors.

Hidden mysteries:

- A sealed statue of Hestia beneath the village.
- An old battlefield under the wheat fields.
- The player's childhood home is built over a Pactbearer tomb.

### Thalassion Sea

Description: Island-dotted ocean with storms, shipwrecks, coral ruins, and drowned temples.

Mood: Wonder, loneliness, open adventure.

Story purpose: Opens the world and reveals that entire civilizations were swallowed.

Unique mechanics:

- Sailing.
- Wind direction.
- Diving bells.
- Whirlpool gates.
- Tide-based cave access.

Hidden mysteries:

- Drowned bells that ring only at moonrise.
- A city visible under clear water but unreachable until late game.
- A living island that is actually a sleeping Primordial scale.

### Nysa Wilds

Description: Dense sacred forest, moonlit glades, hunter villages, old animal shrines.

Mood: Lush, secretive, watchful.

Story purpose: Explores Artemis, wild boundaries, and the difference between protection and control.

Unique mechanics:

- Tracking.
- Silent movement.
- Animal guidance.
- Moon-phase doors.

Hidden mysteries:

- A village that appears only when no weapon is drawn.
- A white stag that leads to optional trials.
- Trees that remember human names.

### Daedalan Isles

Description: Windy islands full of ancient machinery, bronze towers, lifts, mirrors, and workshops.

Mood: Clever, bright, melancholic.

Story purpose: Reveals mortal invention rivaled divine power.

Unique mechanics:

- Gear puzzles.
- Light reflection.
- Gliding.
- Magnetized bronze platforms.

Hidden mysteries:

- Hephaestus's abandoned apprentice.
- A mechanical bird carrying lost letters.
- Blueprints for the Aegis Meridian.

### Kartha Nemea

Description: Ruined lion-gate city half-buried in red grass and cracked stone.

Mood: Majestic, tragic, dangerous.

Story purpose: Shows the cost of heroic mythmaking.

Unique mechanics:

- Heavy gauntlets.
- Ancient arena trials.
- Crumbling wall traversal.
- Truth-based statue ordering.

Hidden mysteries:

- The "monster" once worshiped as a city guardian.
- A royal archive that contradicts temple histories.
- The first hint that heroes rewrote history.

### Hyperion Desert

Description: Golden desert, glass ruins, buried libraries, sun temples, mirage cities.

Mood: Vast, radiant, mournful.

Story purpose: Centers Apollo, prophecy, and the danger of perfect knowledge.

Unique mechanics:

- Sun-shadow puzzles.
- Heat management.
- Mirage navigation.
- Time-of-day gates.

Hidden mysteries:

- A library whose books are written in future tense.
- An oracle who remembers deaths that have not happened.
- A buried observatory tracking Olympus's disappearance.

### Acheron Marsh and Underways

Description: Misty wetlands, black rivers, bone-white trees, entrances to the Underworld.

Mood: Somber, beautiful, sacred.

Story purpose: Reveals Hades is not evil and death is memory's guardian.

Unique mechanics:

- Ferry routes.
- Spirit-name restoration.
- Crossing between living and dead room states.
- Echo conversations.

Hidden mysteries:

- Dead heroes ashamed of their legends.
- A river that erases equipped memories unless protected.
- The name of the player's first incarnation.

### Atlas Spine

Description: Snow mountains, storm peaks, eagle monasteries, frozen giant bones.

Mood: Awe, hardship, divine distance.

Story purpose: Final terrestrial ascent toward Olympus.

Unique mechanics:

- Climbing.
- Wind riding.
- Avalanche triggers.
- Lightning rods.

Hidden mysteries:

- Zeus's first mortal shrine.
- A monastery preserving forbidden hymns.
- A frozen Titan who is not dead.

### Olympus Broken

Description: Floating ruins of divine palaces, storm bridges, memory chambers, impossible gardens.

Mood: Sacred, devastated, sublime.

Story purpose: Final act and moral reckoning.

Unique mechanics:

- All relics combined.
- Reality-shifting puzzles.
- Divine trials based on choices.
- Ending path locks.

Hidden mysteries:

- The gods' fear.
- The player's original failure.
- The Aegis Meridian's true purpose.

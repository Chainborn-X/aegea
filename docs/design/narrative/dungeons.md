# Dungeon Design

## Dungeon Design Rules

- Each major dungeon must have a clear mythic identity, a mechanical identity, and a historical contradiction.
- The dungeon relic must affect combat, traversal, puzzle-solving, and exploration outside the dungeon.
- The final room should reveal something that changes how the player understands the region.
- Bosses should be expressions of history, not only combat gates.

## 1. Hearthwood Shrine

Theme: Home, fire, first memory.

Backstory: A Hestian shrine buried beneath village roots.

Layout concept: Grove exterior, underground hearth halls, root-choked sanctum.

Progression mechanics:

- light braziers
- carry flame through safe paths
- burn memory-thorns

Signature puzzle: Recreate a family meal from ghostly clues to open the central hearth.

Mini boss: Rootbound Shrine Warden.

Final boss: The Ashen Keeper.

Dungeon relic: **Ember of Hestia**.

Narrative purpose: Teaches that places remember love and loss.

## 2. Temple of the Drowned Trident

Theme: Sea, grief, submerged civilization.

Backstory: Poseidon's temple sank during the Sundering.

Layout concept: Tidal chambers, flooded halls, coral sanctuary, bell tower.

Progression mechanics:

- raise and lower water
- use currents as pathways
- ring drowned bells to shift rooms

Signature puzzle: Align three tide bells while water levels change.

Mini boss: Siren Echo.

Final boss: Ketos of the Deep Bell.

Dungeon relic: **Conch of Tides**.

Narrative purpose: Reveals that Poseidon tried to hold back the Sundering and failed.

## 3. Moonroot Labyrinth

Theme: Wild boundaries, moonlight, transformation.

Backstory: Artemis's forest temple, built where humans and beasts once made peace.

Layout concept: Shifting forest maze, burrows, canopy bridges, moonlit sanctum.

Progression mechanics:

- track footprints
- move silently
- open paths under moonbeams

Signature puzzle: Follow the white stag without startling it.

Mini boss: Thorn-Masked Huntress.

Final boss: Kallisto's Remnant.

Dungeon relic: **Moonbow Charm**.

Narrative purpose: Challenges the idea that civilization is always progress.

## 4. The Bronze Labyrinth

Theme: Craft, invention, abandoned tools.

Backstory: Hephaestus's mortal workshop where divine machines were prototyped.

Layout concept: Gear halls, forge chambers, mirror lifts, automaton core.

Progression mechanics:

- rotate machinery
- reflect light
- power elevators
- command small automatons

Signature puzzle: Build a moving bridge from rotating gear segments.

Mini boss: Twin Furnace Sentinels.

Final boss: Talos Fragment.

Dungeon relic: **Gauntlet of Hephaestus**.

Narrative purpose: Shows mortals helped build the device that broke the world.

## 5. The Lion Vault

Theme: Heroism, propaganda, buried betrayal.

Backstory: A royal vault beneath Kartha Nemea containing the true account of a "monster-slaying."

Layout concept: Arena ruins, royal crypts, oath chambers, guardian lair.

Progression mechanics:

- break cracked walls
- move heavy statues
- survive arena trials with altered rules

Signature puzzle: Arrange hero statues according to truth, not legend.

Mini boss: Oathbound Champion.

Final boss: The Nemean Guardian.

Dungeon relic: **Lionheart Aegis**.

Narrative purpose: Reveals that history was edited by victors and priests.

## 6. Sunken Library of Apollo

Theme: Prophecy, truth, unbearable knowledge.

Backstory: Apollo's desert archive sank beneath sand after recording futures the gods feared.

Layout concept: Sand-filled halls, rotating mirrors, silent reading rooms, solar clock tower.

Progression mechanics:

- redirect sunlight
- read partial prophecies
- navigate mirages
- open doors by casting shadows

Signature puzzle: Solve a room by choosing which future not to read.

Mini boss: Mirage Self.

Final boss: The Blind Oracle.

Dungeon relic: **Lyre of Dawn**.

Narrative purpose: Shows prophecy can imprison choice.

## 7. Palace Beneath the River

Theme: Death, names, remembrance.

Backstory: Hades's river palace, fractured when the dead lost their roads.

Layout concept: Ferries, river locks, memorial halls, Lethe crossings, throne garden.

Progression mechanics:

- restore names to spirits
- cross between living and dead versions of rooms
- use memory anchors to resist forgetting

Signature puzzle: Rebuild a dead family's lineage from grave markers and lullabies.

Mini boss: Lethe Knight.

Final boss: The Eater of Names.

Dungeon relic: **Obol of Hades**.

Narrative purpose: Reveals the player's original identity and the gods' buried guilt.

## 8. The Storm Crown

Theme: Sovereignty, fear, divine authority.

Backstory: Zeus's first and last mountain temple.

Layout concept: Storm cliffs, lightning towers, eagle halls, frozen tribunal.

Progression mechanics:

- redirect lightning
- ride wind streams
- ground divine charges
- survive judgment trials

Signature puzzle: Refuse false binary judgments to open the true path.

Mini boss: The Thunder Judge.

Final boss: The First Thunder.

Dungeon relic: **Bolt of Keraunos**.

Narrative purpose: Prepares the player to confront Zeus as a tragic, dangerous remnant.

## 9. Olympus Broken

Theme: Memory, responsibility, final choice.

Backstory: The shattered divine realm.

Layout concept: Connected god-palaces, broken Meridian core, summit of names.

Progression mechanics:

- combine all relics
- repair or dissolve memory chains
- enter gods' Echo chambers
- shape final path through prior choices

Signature puzzle: Reconstruct the First Naming without making it a prison.

Mini boss: Lysandra or Melanthos, depending on player choices.

Final boss: Zeus the Unremembered, followed by Aion's trial.

Dungeon relic: None. The player becomes the final relic.

Narrative purpose: Forces the final decision about gods, mortals, and change.

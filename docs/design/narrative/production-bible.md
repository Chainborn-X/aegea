# Production Bible

## Core Themes

- Memory shapes identity.
- Legacy can guide or imprison.
- Change is frightening but necessary.
- Gods need responsibility as much as mortals do.
- Freedom without memory becomes arrogance.
- Preservation without change becomes death.
- The past must be faced, not simply restored.

## Creative Pillars

- Wonder.
- Exploration.
- Mystery.
- Ancient history.
- Discovery.
- Emotional storytelling.
- Meaningful progression.

## World Summary

Aegea is a post-divine world of islands, ruined kingdoms, sacred forests, drowned temples, desert libraries, underworld rivers, and broken heavenly roads. The gods have faded after the Sundering, a cataclysm caused by a failed attempt to preserve divine order forever. As memory returns, so do monsters, truths, and Primordial powers older than Olympus.

## Story Summary

The player begins as a village survivor and becomes the reincarnated Pactbearer. The quest to restore the gods becomes a quest to understand why the gods vanished, why the Primordials are returning, and whether the old order deserves to return.

The final choice determines whether the world restores Olympus, creates a new pact, ends the gods, or accepts an open future beyond fixed ages.

## Major Characters

- **Hero / Echo-Bearer**: Reincarnated Pactbearer who decides the next age.
- **Ione**: Historian companion seeking truth.
- **Lysandra**: Devout rival knight trying to restore the gods.
- **Phaon**: Sailor companion tied to sea grief.
- **Demeas**: Village elder hiding old guilt.
- **Melanthos**: Mortal king seeking to replace divine rule with human empire.
- **Zeus**: Fading king of gods, tragic final antagonist.
- **Hestia, Athena, Hades, Poseidon, Apollo, Artemis, Hephaestus**: Faded gods whose Echoes reveal parts of the truth.
- **Aion**: Primordial Time and final philosophical force.

## Region Summary

- **Helikon Vale**: home, hearth, first ruins.
- **Thalassion Sea**: sailing, drowned kingdoms, Poseidon.
- **Nysa Wilds**: forest pacts, Artemis, wild mystery.
- **Daedalan Isles**: craft, machines, Hephaestus, Meridian clues.
- **Kartha Nemea**: heroic lies, ruined city, guardian beast.
- **Hyperion Desert**: Apollo, prophecy, buried knowledge.
- **Acheron Marsh**: Hades, death, names, Underworld.
- **Atlas Spine**: Zeus, storm, final ascent.
- **Olympus Broken**: divine ruin and final choice.

## Dungeon Summary

1. **Hearthwood Shrine**: fire and memory.
2. **Temple of the Drowned Trident**: tides and grief.
3. **Moonroot Labyrinth**: stealth and moon paths.
4. **Bronze Labyrinth**: machines and craft.
5. **Lion Vault**: strength and historical truth.
6. **Sunken Library**: light, shadow, prophecy.
7. **Palace Beneath the River**: death and names.
8. **Storm Crown**: lightning and judgment.
9. **Olympus Broken**: final synthesis.

## Progression Summary

Progression is based on:

- relics from dungeons
- new traversal abilities
- forgotten languages
- myth truth collections
- divine blessings
- weapon mastery
- companion arcs
- optional secret knowledge

The player grows by uncovering the world, not by grinding it.

## Production Rules

- Every region contains a visible mystery.
- Every dungeon reveals a historical contradiction.
- Every relic changes combat, traversal, and storytelling.
- Every god is both wondrous and flawed.
- Every monster has a reason to exist.
- Exploration should feel like archaeology, pilgrimage, and adventure.
- The final choice must feel earned by what the player discovered.

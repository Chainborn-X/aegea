# Enemy Ecosystem

## Enemy Design Rules

- Enemies should have ecological, mythic, or historical reasons to exist.
- Combat behavior should express lore where possible.
- Regional enemies should teach local mechanics.
- Elite and boss enemies should recontextualize myths, not only increase difficulty.

## Common Enemies

### Thorn Wights

Lore: Plants grown through old bones.

Behavior: Ambush from grass and roots.

Combat mechanics: Vulnerable to fire and exposed when braziers are lit.

### Cracked Urn Spirits

Lore: Household spirits trapped in broken burial urns.

Behavior: Float, throw shards, retreat into pottery.

Combat mechanics: Can be pacified by restoring shrine flames.

### Oath Shades

Lore: Dead warriors bound to promises no one remembers.

Behavior: Shielded melee patrols.

Combat mechanics: Become weaker when their oath is discovered.

### Brine Crawlers

Lore: Shore creatures mutated by drowned Echoes.

Behavior: Sideways rush, shell guard, burrow in wet sand.

Combat mechanics: Exposed after tide changes.

## Regional Enemies

### Moon Wolves

Lore: Forest hunters that learned human fear during broken pacts.

Behavior: Pack circling and flank attacks.

Combat mechanics: Punish noisy movement; can be calmed with moon rites.

### Glass Scorpions

Lore: Desert predators altered by Apollo's shattered light.

Behavior: Ambush from sand, refract projectiles.

Combat mechanics: Vulnerable when their mirrored shell is turned away from sunlight.

### Gear Sentries

Lore: Daedalan workshop defenders following damaged instructions.

Behavior: Pattern patrols, lock-on charges, area denial.

Combat mechanics: Predictable but layered; can be rewired with Machine Glyph knowledge.

### Lethe Drifters

Lore: Spirits dissolved by the river of forgetting.

Behavior: Slow pursuit, sudden memory-drain lunge.

Combat mechanics: Temporarily erase map markings or ability prompts if they strike.

### Frost Harpies

Lore: Mountain storm scavengers nesting in abandoned lightning chapels.

Behavior: Dive attacks and gust positioning.

Combat mechanics: Grounded by lightning rods or wind timing.

## Mythological Creatures

### Siren Echoes

Lore: Not living sirens, but songs left behind by the drowned.

Behavior: Sing from off-screen, retreat through flooded corridors.

Combat mechanics: Pull the player toward hazards unless countered by sound or bells.

### Lamia Masks

Lore: Possessed ritual masks from abandoned festivals.

Behavior: Leap between empty bodies.

Combat mechanics: Must be stunned by mirror light before damage applies.

### Cyclopean Remnants

Lore: Ancient builders altered by Primordial stone.

Behavior: Slow, huge, territorial.

Combat mechanics: Eye-stun timing, terrain smashing, puzzle-combat arenas.

### Chimera Spawn

Lore: Unstable beasts formed from conflicting myths.

Behavior: Change body language and attacks by phase.

Combat mechanics: Shift weaknesses when damaged.

## Elite Enemies

### Temple Judges

Lore: Divine constructs built to test worthy petitioners.

Behavior: Controlled, ceremonial, rule-bound.

Combat mechanics: Each judge tests a relic mastery pattern.

### Hero Shades

Lore: Ghosts of legendary warriors whose stories are incomplete.

Behavior: Use named weapon techniques.

Combat mechanics: Unlock player techniques after defeat or reconciliation.

### Meridian Knights

Lore: Melanthos's soldiers using salvaged divine machinery.

Behavior: Formation fighting with devices.

Combat mechanics: Mix human tactics with unstable relic effects.

## World Bosses

### Gaia's Root-Heart

Lore: A buried Primordial organ beneath Helikon.

Behavior: Grows, retreats, and reshapes the arena.

Combat mechanics: Arena changes as roots grow and decay.

### The Wandering Colossus

Lore: A broken automaton walking between islands long after its war ended.

Behavior: Slow overworld movement, climbable boss body.

Combat mechanics: Disable parts mid-battle, then enter the core.

### The Storm-Eaten Eagle

Lore: Zeus's old messenger devoured by abandoned thunder.

Behavior: Aerial dominance and lightning dives.

Combat mechanics: Wind positioning, lightning grounding, high-risk counter windows.

## Secret Bosses

### The Hero Who Lied

Lore: The celebrated founder of Kartha Nemea.

Behavior: Begins with flawless heroic attacks, then breaks into fear-driven patterns.

Combat mechanics: Fake heroic attacks until truth phases reveal cowardice and grief.

### The Automaton That Prays

Lore: A machine that kept worshiping after its god vanished.

Behavior: Alternates ritual stillness with machine precision.

Combat mechanics: Mechanical patterns interrupted by unpredictable divine miracles.

### Nyx's Unlit Child

Lore: A dream that never entered the world.

Behavior: Avoids direct sight and removes certainty from the fight.

Combat mechanics: Removes light, sound, and UI clues in phases.

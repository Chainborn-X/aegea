# Complete World History

## Cosmology

The world is called **Aegea**, a land of islands, ruined continents, sacred mountains, drowned kingdoms, underworld rivers, and broken roads to heaven.

Existence has five layers:

- **The Mortal World**: the visible world of humans, beasts, ruins, towns, forests, seas, and kingdoms.
- **Olympus**: not only a mountain, but a divine axis of law, memory, names, and heavenly order.
- **The Underworld**: a subterranean and spiritual realm where the dead move through rivers of memory.
- **The Echo**: the invisible resonance of all things that have lived, died, been loved, forgotten, built, destroyed, or dreamed.
- **The Deep Before**: the primordial state beneath creation, older than gods, language, death, and time.

Magic is not raw energy. Magic is **remembrance made active**. To use magic is to call upon an Echo: a fragment of what once was, what should have been, or what still dreams beneath reality.

## Creation Myth - Official History

Temple scripture teaches that before the world there was chaos.

From chaos arose the **Primordials**: Earth, Sea, Sky, Night, Flame, Time, and Silence. Their dreams were vast and shapeless. They birthed storms, mountains, beasts, stars, and death, but the world had no mercy, memory, or order.

Then came the Olympian gods.

- Zeus split the storm.
- Poseidon calmed the sea.
- Demeter taught the soil to bear grain.
- Apollo gave light meaning.
- Athena gave thought shape.
- Hades gave death a road.
- Hermes gave language to distance.
- Hestia gave mortals the hearth.

The gods bound the violent Primordials, raised Olympus, and guided humanity into civilization.

## Creation Myth - Hidden Truth

The Primordials were not evil. They were the first principles of existence: vast, impersonal, necessary.

The gods were not creators. They were born from humanity's first collective act of remembrance.

When early mortals gathered under a sky of living storms, they named the storm, the sea, the hearth, the hunt, the grave, and the harvest. These names became vessels. The vessels became gods.

The Olympians were shaped by mortal memory, then grew powerful enough to shape mortals in return.

The divine order was a pact:

- Mortals gave memory, worship, story, and names.
- Gods gave protection, structure, meaning, and continuity.
- The Primordials remained bound beneath reality because they were reality's roots, not enemies to be destroyed.

## Origin of the Gods

The Olympians emerged from the **First Naming**, an ancient era when humanity gave form to forces it could not survive alone.

Each god is an idea given personhood:

- **Zeus**: sovereignty, storm, judgment.
- **Hera**: covenant, lineage, duty.
- **Poseidon**: depth, change, wrath.
- **Demeter**: cycles, nourishment, grief.
- **Athena**: wisdom, craft, strategy.
- **Apollo**: prophecy, music, healing, truth.
- **Artemis**: wildness, boundaries, moonlight.
- **Ares**: conflict, courage, ruin.
- **Aphrodite**: desire, beauty, longing.
- **Hermes**: roads, thieves, language, thresholds.
- **Hephaestus**: invention, fire, imperfection.
- **Dionysus**: ecstasy, transformation, divine madness.
- **Hades**: death, wealth, remembrance beneath the earth.
- **Hestia**: home, continuity, quiet endurance.

The gods are strongest when remembered clearly. As history decayed after the Sundering, so did they.

## Origin of Humanity

Official myth says Prometheus stole divine fire and gave mortals intelligence.

The hidden truth is stranger: Prometheus did not steal fire from Olympus. He recovered it from the Primordial Flame and taught mortals to carry it without being consumed.

This fire became:

- craft
- ambition
- rebellion
- civilization
- free will

Because of this, humanity was never fully owned by the gods.

## Origin of Monsters

Monsters arise from four sources:

- **Primordial seepage**: ancient forces leaking into living forms.
- **Broken Echoes**: memories trapped in grief, war, betrayal, or forgotten rituals.
- **Divine remnants**: beasts once blessed or cursed by gods, now warped by abandonment.
- **Human hubris**: experiments, weapons, kings, and cults that tried to command forces they did not understand.

Most monsters are not random evil. They are symptoms of historical wounds.

## Origin of Magic

Magic is called **Echo-craft**.

It has three primary practices:

- **Invocation**: calling on gods, spirits, or remembered names.
- **Resonance**: awakening dormant memories in places, objects, and bloodlines.
- **Pactcraft**: binding oneself to a law, relic, god, or Primordial principle.

Magic faded after the Sundering because people forgot the old languages, true names, sacred songs, and histories that made magic stable.

## The Primordials

The Primordials are not ordinary villains. They are ancient realities returning after being suppressed.

- **Gaia, the Rooting Earth**: life without hierarchy; growth, burial, hunger, renewal.
- **Pontos, the Unbounded Sea**: depth, drowning, freedom, dissolution.
- **Ouranos, the Starless Sky**: vastness, observation, distance, cosmic silence.
- **Nyx, the First Night**: dreams, fear, mercy, secrets, sleep.
- **Aion, the Coiled Time**: recurrence, fate, aging, memory loops.
- **Phanes, the Unshaped Light**: birth, possibility, unbearable revelation.
- **Erebos, the Hollow Beneath**: absence, erasure, forgotten names.

The gods once bound these beings through the **Pact of Olympus**, but the pact was never meant to last forever.

## The Sundering - Official History

The Sundering is remembered as a war between gods and Titans. Olympus sealed itself away to protect mortals. The gods faded heroically, leaving temples, relics, and blessings behind.

## The Sundering - Hidden Truth

The Sundering was caused when Zeus and several gods attempted to make the Olympian order permanent.

They feared that if mortals changed too much, the gods would lose form and fade. To preserve themselves, they built the **Aegis Meridian**, a divine mechanism that would fix memory, history, and worship in place.

Athena, Hestia, Hermes, Hades, and Prometheus opposed it. Mortals were not meant to be preserved like statues.

The mechanism failed. It shattered the connection between Olympus, the Underworld, and the Mortal World.

Consequences:

- the gods faded into fragments
- temples lost power
- seas rose and drowned old kingdoms
- Underworld rivers overflowed into caves and marshes
- monsters multiplied
- human history collapsed into legend
- the Primordials began to stir

## Timeline

### Age of First Breath

- Humanity awakens in a dangerous world.
- The First Naming gives birth to divine forms.

### Age of Hearths

- Hestia teaches settlement.
- Demeter teaches cultivation.
- Hermes teaches roads and trade.

### Age of Bronze Stars

- Great city-states rise.
- Magic, astronomy, shipbuilding, and templecraft flourish.

### Age of the Divine Pact

- Gods and mortals seal the Primordials beneath the world.
- The Pactbearers are created: mortal souls able to mediate between gods, humans, and Primordials.

### Age of Heroes

- Heroes slay monsters, found kingdoms, and build wonders.
- Many later "monster slayings" were suppressions of Primordial wounds.

### Age of the Aegis Meridian

- Zeus begins the secret project to preserve divine order forever.
- Dissent divides Olympus.

### The Sundering

- The Meridian ruptures.
- Olympus vanishes.
- The Underworld floods upward.
- The world loses much of its memory.

### The Quiet Centuries

- Humanity survives in scattered towns and islands.
- Temples become ruins.
- Gods become folktales.

### Present Age: The Age of Echoes

- Ancient ruins awaken.
- Forgotten languages return in dreams.
- The player's soul reincarnates.
- The last divine pact begins to call.

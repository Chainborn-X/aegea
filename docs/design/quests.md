# Quests

## Quest Philosophy

Quests should feel like reasons to explore, not task lists. The player should often learn what to do through place, rumor, object, and consequence.

## Quest Forms

- Landmark investigation.
- Shrine restoration.
- Missing route discovery.
- Local legend verification.
- Relic recovery.
- Creature behavior observation.
- Environmental puzzle chain.
- Journey to a visible distant place.

## Writing Style

Use concise, grounded language. Let NPCs speak from their place and experience. Avoid modern quips, long exposition dumps, and quest text that solves the mystery before the player acts.

## Quest Marking

Prefer:

- Map sketches.
- Rumor notes.
- Landmarks.
- Environmental signs.
- Journal fragments.

Avoid:

- Dense objective checklists.
- Constant arrow guidance.
- Overly explicit UI instructions.

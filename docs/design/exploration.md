# Exploration

## Exploration Promise

The player should constantly ask:

> Can I go there?

The game should often answer yes, sometimes answer later, and occasionally answer not the way you expected.

## Exploration Tools

Potential traversal and discovery tools:

- Climbing or ledge traversal.
- Swimming, diving, or small-boat travel.
- Light sources for caves and sealed rooms.
- Keys, relics, songs, blessings, or offerings.
- Environmental reading, such as recognizing old symbols.
- Weather, tide, sunlight, or time-based access.

## World Layout

The world should favor interconnected routes, visible landmarks, and optional detours. Avoid turning every region into a straight sequence of combat rooms.

## Secrets

Secrets should be fair. The player should be able to infer them from:

- Unusual terrain.
- Repeated visual language.
- Sound cues.
- NPC rumor fragments.
- Inscriptions.
- Map irregularities.
- Visible unreachable objects or paths.

## Rewards

Discovery rewards can include:

- New paths.
- Relics.
- Lore fragments.
- Abilities.
- Crafting materials.
- Music motifs.
- Shrine blessings.
- Companions or temporary guides, used sparingly.

# Game Design

## Core Loop

Explore, notice, travel, fight, discover, return with new understanding, and push farther into the world.

The player should frequently alternate between:

- Seeing something intriguing.
- Finding a route toward it.
- Encountering resistance or mystery.
- Gaining knowledge, items, abilities, or access.
- Recontextualizing earlier places.

## Player Experience

The first hours should establish:

- Alone near the sea.
- Open routes rather than a single corridor.
- Natural tutorial through environment and interaction.
- Visible long-term landmarks.
- Early secrets that reward curiosity.
- Combat as part of travel, not the only reason to move.

## Design Priorities

- Exploration readability.
- Environmental storytelling.
- Action RPG immediacy.
- Meaningful traversal progression.
- Secrets that feel placed in the world, not hidden by arbitrary rules.
- Quiet pacing between intense encounters.

## Design Risks

- Overloading the player with lore too early.
- Making the world feel like disconnected biomes.
- Replacing discovery with checklist completion.
- Letting combat density overwhelm solitude.
- Making Greek inspiration too literal or too generic.

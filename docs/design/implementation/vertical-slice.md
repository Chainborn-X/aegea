# Vertical Slice Plan

The first vertical slice should prove that Echoes of Olympus can deliver wonder, exploration, melancholy beauty, and meaningful progression in a compact playable arc.

## Slice Title

**The Star in the Hearthwood**

## Target Experience

The player should feel:

- protected by a familiar village
- drawn toward a visible mystery
- unsettled by ruins under ordinary life
- rewarded for curiosity
- changed by the first relic
- invited to revisit old spaces with new understanding

## Playable Scope

### Required Locations

- Elia village.
- North cliff ruins.
- Hearthwood approach.
- Hearthwood Shrine.
- Pactbearer crypt teaser.
- One optional cave or basement opened after the relic.

### Required NPCs

- Demeas.
- Widow of Elia.
- Shrine child or local rumor source.
- Hestia Echo.
- 3-5 village NPCs with post-event state changes.

### Required Enemies

- Thorn Wight.
- Cracked Urn Spirit.
- Rootbound Shrine Warden mini boss.
- Ashen Keeper boss.

### Required Items

- Bronze Shortblade.
- Ember of Hestia.
- Honey Cakes.
- Widow's Bell.
- First myth fragment.

## Slice Flow

### Beat 1 - Dream of the Gate

Purpose: Establish mystery and long-term landmark.

Gameplay: Short playable dream corridor or controlled vignette.

Exit condition: Player wakes in Elia.

### Beat 2 - Elia Morning

Purpose: Establish home before danger.

Gameplay: Exploration, basic interactions, optional NPC conversations.

Required discoveries:

- sealed cliff ruin is visible
- village hearth is important
- Demeas is hiding concern

### Beat 3 - Falling Star

Purpose: Inciting incident.

Gameplay: Short event, then player control resumes.

Required state change:

- Hearthwood route opens
- first enemies appear
- village NPCs move to crisis positions

### Beat 4 - First Combat Route

Purpose: Teach combat through travel.

Gameplay: Sword acquisition, Thorn Wight encounter, simple blocked routes.

Required tutorial rule: Teach through level layout and feedback, not text-heavy prompts.

### Beat 5 - Hearthwood Shrine

Purpose: First dungeon.

Gameplay:

- light puzzles
- root barriers
- urn spirits
- central hearth puzzle
- mini boss
- relic acquisition

Required reveal:

- shrine was not abandoned by accident
- it protected a buried memory of the Sundering

### Beat 6 - Ember Mastery

Purpose: Let the relic reshape the dungeon.

Gameplay:

- return through prior rooms using Ember
- reveal memory overlays
- open new route to boss

### Beat 7 - Ashen Keeper

Purpose: First boss as emotional history.

Gameplay:

- fire safe zones
- root waves
- urn spirit summons
- vulnerability after relighting hearths

Narrative beat:

- The boss is a guardian that forgot who it protected.

### Beat 8 - Return to Elia

Purpose: Recontextualize home.

Gameplay:

- village state changes
- Demeas reveals the soul-mark
- optional hidden passage under a home opens
- widow side story progresses if started

Required final image:

- distant Olympus-like storm mountain appears or is hinted in the sky.

## Systems Proven

- top-down movement
- sword combat
- basic enemy AI
- dialogue
- interactables
- dungeon room progression
- relic ability
- persistent flags
- hidden passage reveal
- memory/Echo scene
- boss encounter
- post-dungeon world state

## Slice Completion Criteria

The slice is ready when:

- a new player can complete it without developer guidance
- the first relic clearly changes how the player reads the world
- Elia feels worth protecting
- the shrine feels ancient rather than merely themed
- at least one secret is visible before it is reachable
- at least one NPC line changes after the boss
- at least one optional action creates a later flag

## Cut Scope If Needed

If time is limited, cut in this order:

1. optional cave or basement
2. extra village NPCs
3. mini boss
4. dream sequence interactivity

Do not cut:

- relic use
- memory reveal
- post-dungeon return state
- boss as forgotten guardian

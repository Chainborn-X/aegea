# Aegea Vision

## High Concept

Aegea is a top-down action RPG about exploring a forgotten mythological Mediterranean world inspired by ancient Greece.

The player begins alone near the sea. There is no crowded tutorial village, no immediate party, and no dense exposition. The first promise is space: coastline, wind, ruins, birds, water, distant landforms, and the possibility that any visible path might lead somewhere meaningful.

## Emotional Core

> I am alone. The world is vast. Something incredible is waiting beyond the next hill.

This sentence is the creative test for the project. Mechanics, art, music, writing, level design, UI, pacing, and technical choices should support it.

## Pillars

### Wonder

The world should feel larger than the player understands. Ancient structures, unreachable landmarks, mythic creatures, and distant islands should suggest histories and possibilities that are not immediately explained.

### Discovery

Exploration should reveal useful paths, secrets, lore, items, characters, and vistas. Discovery should be frequent enough to reward curiosity but quiet enough to preserve mystery.

### Freedom

The world should invite self-directed exploration. The player should often see several plausible routes and choose based on curiosity rather than explicit instruction.

### Solitude

Solitude is not loneliness as despair. It is the feeling of being a lone traveler in a large, beautiful, ancient place.

### Adventure

The game should keep movement, danger, and surprise alive. Exploration should include caves, ruins, cliffs, temples, islands, hidden passages, and strange encounters.

### Mystery

The world should contain traces of forgotten gods, vanished peoples, sealed places, and unexplained phenomena. Answers should be earned through exploration.

### Hope

Even dangerous or ruined places should retain light, beauty, and possibility. Aegea should not collapse into cynicism or grimdark fatalism.

## Inspirations

Aegea draws inspiration from:

- Battle of Olympus: mythic adventure, ancient atmosphere, and journey structure.
- Secret of Mana: lush environments, color, wonder, and readable action RPG presentation.
- Chrono Trigger: clarity, charm, environmental storytelling, and purposeful world variety.
- The Legend of Zelda: exploration, secrets, traversal, and curiosity-driven progression.

These references are not templates to copy. They identify the desired emotional territory.

## Creative Boundaries

Aegea is not historical Greece. It is a mythological world inspired by Greece.

Avoid:

- Generic medieval castles, taverns, knights, and feudal fantasy shorthand.
- Heavy anime facial language, exaggerated expressions, or modern anime styling.
- Cartoonish proportions or comedic world presentation.
- Dark fantasy decay as the dominant tone.
- Overexplained lore.
- Worlds that feel arranged as attractions instead of discovered places.

Prefer:

- Marble ruins broken by roots, vines, moss, flowers, and weather.
- Sunlit coastlines, turquoise water, dusty hills, cypress silhouettes, olive groves, and sacred springs.
- Mythological creatures that feel ancient and regionally grounded.
- Quiet moments between danger.
- Distant landmarks that create goals without quest markers.

# Environment Guide

## Scene Composition

Every environment should answer three questions:

- Where can I go now?
- What might I reach later?
- What happened here before I arrived?

## Required Scene Hooks

Most screens should include at least one:

- Visible but inaccessible path.
- Distant landmark.
- Cave entrance.
- Ruined doorway.
- Unusual tree, statue, altar, or spring.
- Change in terrain that suggests a secret.
- Environmental contrast, such as sunlight entering a dark cave.

## Biome Notes

### Coast

Use bright water, clean silhouettes, gulls, shore rocks, shells, grasses, and ruin fragments. Establish the feeling of beginning at the edge of a huge world.

Use turquoise water, white foam, rocky cliffs, flowering grasses, seabirds, sea caves, ruins, and distant islands. The coast is the emotional opening statement of Aegea.

### Sea Cave

Use wet stone, reflected turquoise light, tide pools, mineral streaks, and partial darkness. Caves should feel cool and mysterious without becoming visually flat.

Use strong contrast between darkness and light. Include torch glow, glowing minerals, underground rivers, wet stone, roots, and hidden chambers.

### Forest

Use Mediterranean tree species, broken old paths, filtered light, and clear traversal lanes. Avoid generic dense fantasy woods.

Use dappled sunlight, thick greenery, mossy stones, ancient paths, hidden shrines, and sacred clearings.

### Sacred Grove

Use old trees, springs, flowers, offerings, stone rings, and calm light. These spaces should feel protected and watched.

### Marsh

Use reeds, raised ruins, shallow reflections, old causeways, insects, and mist. Keep navigation readable.

Use fog, reeds, dark reflective water, half-sunken ruins, twisted trees, and muted green/brown palettes with small supernatural highlights.

### Mountain

Use verticality, wind, exposed stone, switchbacks, and distant views. Mountains should sell the player-is-small principle.

Use elevation, cliffs, old paths, wind, distant haze, mountain flowers, ruins carved into stone, and glimpses of faraway regions.

### Temple

Use columns, processional paths, statues, ritual pools, broken roofs, light beams, and plant growth. Temple layouts should imply ceremony and hidden chambers.

Use ivory marble, broken columns, steps, courtyards, vines, flowers, cracked statues, bronze details, sacred geometry, and dramatic light shafts.

### Underworld

Use stillness, pale flowers, quiet water, dim metals, and monumental emptiness. Avoid making it merely hellish.

Use deep blues, cyan light, warm torch contrast, subterranean rivers, ancient gates, black stone, and mythic dread. Avoid pure darkness; preserve beauty.

### Forgotten Village

Use domestic details: wells, ovens, gardens, tools, pottery, textiles, and local shrines. Show absence through what remains.

### Cliffside Ruins

Use wind, elevation, broken stairs, hanging vegetation, and sea views. These areas should create constant curiosity and mild danger.

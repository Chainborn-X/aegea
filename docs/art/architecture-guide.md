# Architecture Guide

## Architectural Identity

Aegea architecture should draw from ancient Greek and broader Mediterranean forms without becoming an archaeological reconstruction.

Use:

- Columns and colonnades.
- Courtyards and terraces.
- Processional steps.
- Marble, limestone, terracotta, bronze, and painted plaster.
- Fountains, channels, cisterns, and ritual pools.
- Cliff-cut shrines and cave sanctuaries.
- Island harbors and sea walls.

## Ruin Language

Ruins should show age through:

- Cracks and missing stones.
- Settling and leaning forms.
- Broken rooflines.
- Displaced columns.
- Faded pigments.
- Roots splitting masonry.
- Flowers and grasses growing in joints.
- Water staining and salt weathering.

## Sacred Spaces

Sacred architecture should be aligned with landscape. Temples may frame sunrise, moonlight, a mountain peak, a spring, a sea channel, or a constellation.

## Avoid

- Medieval castles.
- Gothic cathedrals.
- Generic fantasy towers.
- Perfectly clean white marble everywhere.
- Overly symmetrical levels that feel artificial at gameplay scale.

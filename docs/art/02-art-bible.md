# Art Bible

## Visual Identity

Aegea is a hand-painted top-down action RPG with mythological Mediterranean atmosphere. It should feel sunlit, ancient, lush, mysterious, and adventurous.

Primary visual influences:

- Secret of Mana
- Chrono Trigger
- Battle of Olympus
- Studio Ghibli environments
- Mediterranean coastlines
- Ancient Greek ruins

The art direction should be premium indie RPG: painterly, readable, warm, and full of environmental storytelling.

## Core Principles

### Nature Reclaims Civilization

Ruins should rarely appear pristine. Ancient civilization is visible through broken forms, but nature has softened, invaded, and transformed it.

Include:

- Vines
- Moss
- Roots
- Flowers
- Weathering
- Broken statues
- Cracked marble
- Fallen columns
- Grass between stones

### Sunlit Adventure

Outdoor environments should feel illuminated by a real sun.

Use:

- Warm lighting
- Atmospheric haze
- Strong highlights
- Vibrant water
- Rich vegetation
- Clear shadow direction

Avoid:

- Muddy palettes
- Flat lighting
- Excessive darkness
- Colorless stone

### Player Is Small

The environment should dominate the composition. The player should feel like a small traveler in a large world.

Use:

- Large ruins relative to the player.
- Distant landmarks.
- Oversized trees, cliffs, arches, doors, and statues.
- Compositions that reveal paths beyond the immediate play area.

### Every Scene Invites Discovery

Every screen should contain visual hooks:

- Distant islands
- Cave entrances
- Mountain paths
- Ruins
- Hidden groves
- Unreachable landmarks
- Broken bridges
- Unlit shrine doors

The player should constantly wonder:

> Can I go there?

## Presentation Boundaries

Avoid:

- Grimdark rendering.
- Cartoonish exaggeration.
- Heavy anime character styling.
- Generic medieval fantasy props.
- Theme park staging where every landmark feels conveniently arranged.

Prefer:

- Lush natural irregularity.
- Ancient construction with believable wear.
- Strong readable silhouettes.
- Painterly texture.
- Warm-cool contrast.
- Clear top-down gameplay readability.

# Creatures

## Creature Philosophy

Creatures in Aegea should feel like they belong to mythological Mediterranean landscapes. They are not random monsters placed for combat density. They should imply habitats, legends, behaviors, and relationships to old sacred places.

## Categories

### Natural Creatures

Wild animals and regionally grounded fauna that help the world feel alive.

- Sea birds, goats, foxes, snakes, lizards, fish, bees, crabs, and deer.
- Use for ambient life, environmental cues, and occasional gameplay interactions.

### Mythic Beasts

Creatures shaped by divine influence, old curses, or sacred geography.

- Bronze-feathered birds nesting in temple roofs.
- Root-bound guardians in sacred groves.
- Stone-backed boars in mountain ruins.
- Tide serpents that appear near drowned shrines.
- Lamp-eyed shades in Underworld paths.

### Guardians

Purposeful beings tied to temples, gates, relics, or vows.

- Should have strong silhouettes and ritual context.
- Their arenas should tell the player why they are there.
- Defeating or bypassing them should change access, not merely clear a room.

## Design Rules

- Avoid generic goblins, orcs, skeleton hordes, and medieval bestiary defaults.
- Use marble, bronze, roots, shells, salt, flowers, feathers, reeds, ash, water, and sunlight as material cues.
- Let creatures show age and place through texture.
- Keep designs readable from top-down gameplay scale.

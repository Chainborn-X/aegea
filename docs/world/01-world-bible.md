# World Bible

## Identity

Aegea is a mythological Mediterranean world inspired by ancient Greece, not a historical reconstruction. It borrows the emotional texture of coastlines, islands, temples, ruins, groves, marble, bronze, myth, sea wind, and sunlit stone without requiring historical accuracy.

## Core World Promise

The world is old, beautiful, and only partly understood. Civilizations have risen, built temples into cliffs, crossed the sea, bargained with gods, vanished, and left their works to be reclaimed by nature.

The player arrives after the age of certainty. Roads are broken. Shrines are silent. Some gods are forgotten. Some may still be listening.

## Tonal Rules

- Ruins are mysterious, not merely dead.
- Nature is powerful, generous, and sometimes dangerous.
- The sea is a boundary, a path, and a source of longing.
- Myth is present in the land rather than delivered only through dialogue.
- Beauty should survive even in places of loss.

## World Questions

These questions should guide future development:

- Who built the oldest temples, and why were they abandoned?
- Which gods are forgotten, sleeping, absent, or transformed?
- What lies beyond the visible islands?
- Why does nature overtake some ruins faster than others?
- What did people fear enough to seal beneath mountains and temples?
- Why does the player begin alone near the sea?

## Continuity Rules

- Aegea should feel like one coherent world with regional variation, not unrelated fantasy zones.
- Mythological elements should emerge from place: sea caves, groves, volcanic cliffs, temples, marshes, islands, mountains.
- Names should lean Mediterranean and mythic without directly copying famous historical figures unless intentionally reimagined.
- Lore should invite interpretation. Prefer fragments, inscriptions, local legends, murals, statues, songs, and environmental clues over exposition.

# Factions

## Faction Approach

Factions should support exploration, mystery, and world history. Avoid turning the world into a simple set of political quest hubs. Groups should feel like remnants, custodians, wanderers, cults, craftspeople, or isolated communities shaped by geography and myth.

## Initial Faction Seeds

### The Keepers of the Shore

Quiet coastal caretakers who maintain small sea shrines and read weather, tides, and old inscriptions. They know fragments of routes between islands but rarely speak directly about the gods.

### The Laurel Circle

Pilgrims, herbalists, and seers connected to sacred groves. They preserve songs and plant lore, but their traditions may be incomplete or contradictory.

### The Bronze Wayfarers

Traveling smiths and relic menders who understand ancient alloys, mechanisms, bells, and temple doors. They may help translate material history into gameplay upgrades.

### The Ashen Oath

A severe order guarding sealed mountain and Underworld thresholds. They are not villains by default; they may be protecting the world from a truth no one else remembers.

### Island Households

Small, scattered communities across archipelagos. Each island should have local customs, practical concerns, and myths tied to nearby landmarks.

## Rules

- Factions should create routes, rumors, tools, and mysteries.
- Keep faction identities grounded in landscape and ritual.
- Avoid modern ideological flattening or simple good/evil labeling.

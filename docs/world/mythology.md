# Mythology

## Approach

Aegea uses myth as a living force in the landscape. Gods, spirits, monsters, temples, curses, blessings, and sacred places should feel connected to geography and memory.

The world may echo Greek mythology, but it should not simply retell famous myths. Create original divine figures, local cults, forgotten rites, and regional legends that feel ancient and Mediterranean.

## Divine Presence

Gods in Aegea may be:

- Forgotten by name but still present through places.
- Sleeping beneath temples, mountains, or seas.
- Fragmented into signs, weather, animals, dreams, or relics.
- Misremembered by different regions.
- Hopeful, dangerous, grieving, proud, absent, or transformed.

## Sacred Signs

Use recurring signs to imply divine influence:

- Sudden shafts of sunlight in enclosed ruins.
- Flowers growing where nothing else should.
- Springs flowing through abandoned temples.
- Birds circling over hidden paths.
- Statues facing places the player has not yet reached.
- Murals that change meaning after later discoveries.

## Mythic Tone

Myths should feel old and resonant. Avoid modern sarcasm, parody gods, or overfamiliar fantasy pantheons. Divine stories can contain tragedy, but the dominant tone should remain mysterious and adventurous rather than cruel.
